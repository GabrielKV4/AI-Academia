**Baseline Summary**
- In plants, carbon dioxide (CO2) enters through stomata and diffuses to mesophyll cells, eventually reaching the stroma of chloroplasts where light-independent reactions of photosynthesis occur. These reactions are commonly referred to as the Calvin cycle, named after its discoverer, or the Calvin-Benson cycle, acknowledging another scientist's contribution. The outdated term "dark reaction" is discouraged as it misleadingly suggests that these reactions occur only at night or are entirely independent of light.

- Carbon dioxide (CO2) enters leaves through stomata.
- CO2 diffuses to mesophyll cells in the leaf.
- The stroma of chloroplasts is where reactions occur.
- The Calvin cycle is a key part of photosynthesis.
- "Dark reaction" is an outdated term for these processes.
